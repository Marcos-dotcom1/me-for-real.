Forged in stubbornness—these are my self-built guitars. They're loud, proud, and unapologetically mine.

I could've bought one.

But where's the fun in that? So I built my own—Frankenstein-style. It might bite. It definitely growls. And it sounds like me.

The CMMWE

A classical concept crammed into anything but a classical shape. The CMMWE is all bold outlines and comic-book chaos—black on white, loud by design. It's fretless too. Who asked for this? No idea. But here it is: the Comic Sans of guitars.

The PTST

Inspired by the beauty of Portugal, this one is wrapped in blue and white like the iconic azulejos. Play it under the sun. Play it with class. Pastel de Nata optional (but recommended).

The Encarnacion

Fueled by love, this is a guitar that makes the devil tremble in fear. Equipped with spikes and chains—it's the guitar equivalent of the Cologne Cathedral.

The CMM

Nobody asked for it, but I made it anyway: An electric version of the CMMWE, because chaos deserves amplification.
